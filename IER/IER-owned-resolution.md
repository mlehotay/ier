## **1. Orientation: Resolution Never Pauses**

Every physical system resolves forward in time.

States succeed states.
Possibilities collapse into actualities.
Something happens rather than nothing.

Resolution never pauses — **but it may slow, destabilize, or reorganize**.

This is true whether or not experience exists.

> **Resolution is universal and uninterrupted; what varies is where its cost is borne.**

IER does not explain why resolution occurs.
It explains **when and why resolution becomes owned**, rather than merely occurring.

---

## **2. Inertia: Default Continuation Under Existing Coordination**

Under IER, **inertia** is not habit, laziness, or psychological resistance.

It is:

> **The continuation of coordination that does not yet require additional intrinsic constraint to remain coherent.**

Inertia reflects:

* reuse of established coordination
* minimization of additional intrinsic constraint
* path dependence under existing structure

Inertia is **structural**, not agentive.

It applies:

* to non-experiential systems
* to experiential systems
* and to systems in between

If no additional intrinsic constraint is accepted, **default continuation occurs**.

---

## **3. Bias: Inherited and Evolving Geometry of Viable Futures**

Not all futures are equally sustainable.

Under IER:

> **Bias is the inherited cost landscape that shapes which futures remain viable under intrinsic constraint.**

Bias reflects:

* sedimented coordination
* embodiment
* prior settlements
* asymmetric switching costs

Bias is **not**:

* preference
* desire
* belief
* evaluation
* value

Bias is not produced by deliberation.
It is **inherited from the system’s history and structure**.

Crucially:

> **Bias increases over time as settlement proceeds and switching costs rise.**

Bias is therefore **dynamic**, not static — and increasingly constraining.

---

## **4. Settlement: Gradual Loss of Alternatives**

As resolution unfolds under constraint:

* some futures remain viable
* others lose sustainment

This yields **settlement**:

> **The irreversible collapse of admissible futures through loss of support.**

Settlement is:

* continuous
* non-selective
* not a decision
* not a boundary-crossing event

Alternatives disappear because the regime can no longer sustain them — not because they are rejected.

---

## **5. Closure: Where Irreversibility Must Be Borne**

Resolution and settlement occur everywhere.

What differs is **where their consequences land**.

Under IER:

> **Closure specifies where intrinsic constraint must be borne and cannot be externalized.**

Closure is:

* not the boundary of the physical system
* not the boundary of cognition
* not the boundary of the organism

Closure is the boundary of the **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)**.

Within closure:

* irreversibility accumulates
* cost cannot be offloaded
* future viability is internally constrained

**Clarification**

> Closure is defined *functionally*, not anatomically; embodiment matters only because closure must be physically realized somewhere.

Closure is what makes resolution **owned**.

---

## **6. Embodiment: Physical Realization of Closure**

Closure cannot exist in abstraction.

Intrinsic constraint must be **physically borne**.

This is where embodiment enters — not as identity, but as **medium**.

The body matters because:

* constraint accumulates somewhere
* energy is dissipated somewhere
* fatigue and saturation occur somewhere
* offloading eventually fails somewhere

> **The body is not the self; it is where closure is paid.**

Guardrail:

> Embodiment does not require that all causal contributors lie within the body; it requires only that intrinsic constraint cannot be offloaded beyond the boundary of the UEF.

---

## **7. Parallelism: Choice Emerges During Ongoing Resolution**

Resolution does not complete and then pause for choice.

Time never stops.

> **Choice must originate while the previous moment is still settling.**

Key structural facts:

* bias reshaping begins before settlement completes
* inertia and choice operate **in parallel**, not in sequence
* there is no temporal boundary at which resolution stops and choice begins

> **The two are braided.**

This explains:

* why timing matters
* why hesitation collapses choice
* why last-second effort often fails
* why default actions “just happen”

Choice does not introduce a new resolution event.
It reshapes **how ongoing resolution is persistently borne** under intrinsic constraint.

---

## **8. Choice: Owned Bias Competing with Inertia**

Choice does not interrupt inertia.

Instead:

> **Choice occurs when a unified regime accepts additional intrinsic constraint early enough to reshape bias while default continuation is already unfolding.**

Choice works by:

* recruiting additional processes
* tolerating temporary instability
* sustaining multiple futures longer
* weakening default bias

Choice does **not** select outcomes.

It alters **which future becomes cheapest to continue into**.

This is why choice is sensitive to:

* timing
* fatigue
* stress
* interruption

---

## **9. Why Choice Feels Like a Moment**

Phenomenology tracks **constraint uptake**, not causal duration.

The felt “moment” of choice corresponds to:

* onset of additional intrinsic constraint
* recruitment into participation
* resistance to inertia

> **Choice feels momentary because the onset of constraint uptake is where cost becomes salient, even though its causal influence unfolds over time.**

Phenomenology compresses extended causal influence into a punctual feeling.

This is not illusion.
It is **salience compression**.

The feeling of decisiveness marks **when inertia is first resisted**, not when the future is determined.

---

## **10. Ownership: Causal, Not Retroactive**

Outcomes are not owned after the fact.

Under IER:

> **Ownership is a causal condition, not a narrative attribution.**

When closure is present:

* bias operates internally
* irreversibility constrains the system’s own future
* continuation requires internal reorganization

A result is owned because:

> **the system’s intrinsic constraint structure helped produce it.**

Ownership participates **upstream**, not retroactively.

## **11. Automatic Resolution and Choice-Qualified Resolution**

### **11.1 Automatic Resolution**

* inertia dominates
* bias is extreme
* alternatives never stabilize

Examples:

* fluent speech
* skilled motor sequences
* habitual actions

Resolution occurs.
Choice does not.

**Clarification**

> Automatic resolution may still be owned if it occurs within closure, even though no choice occurs.

---

### **11.2 Choice-Qualified Resolution**

* bias is weak or conflicted
* multiple futures are sustained
* additional intrinsic constraint is accepted
* settlement is redirected

Resolution still occurs —
but it occurs **within closure**, under owned bias.

That is choice.

---

### **11.3 Why It Feels Different From “Choosing”**

Compare two cases.

**Case 1: Greeting response**

* No felt branching
* No deliberative narrowing
* No costful exclusion
* No sense of authorship being constructed

The words just appear — already formed.

That’s because:

* the alternatives never became owned possibilities
* they were never carried as futures you would have had to bear
* because no additional intrinsic constraint was accepted within closure

So nothing had to be settled as choice.

---

**Case 2: A hard decision**

* Futures are explicitly present
* Narrowing is felt as loss
* Delay increases pressure
* Collapse feels irreversible

Afterward: responsibility, regret, relief.

That’s when:

* a genuine choice space exists
* settlement happens with ownership
* and we name that pattern *choice*

Same underlying phenomenon.
Different geometry.

---

**The key distinction (this is important)**

Not all settlement is choice.
But all choice is settlement.

Greeting responses are:

* pre-collapsed
* locally automatic
* **owned without having been choice-qualified**

They become yours because you said them —
not because you chose them.

And that is not a failure.
IER fully allows this.

## **Appendix A — Walkthroughs: Automatic vs. Choice-Qualified Resolution**

This appendix provides two concrete walkthroughs that instantiate the same underlying mechanism—**resolution by loss of alternatives under intrinsic constraint**—in two different geometries.

The goal is not to add cases or diagnostics, but to make explicit **why some owned actions do not feel like choice**, while others do.

---

### **Walkthrough A: Automatic Response (Greeting)**

*Settlement without choice*

> *Someone says: “Hey!”*

---

#### **A1. Ongoing regime (before the greeting finishes)**

* A Unified Experiential Field (UEF) is already mid-trajectory:

  * posture
  * conversational tempo
  * social context
  * affective tone
* Speech production systems are already engaged.
* No suspension occurs.
* No temporal gap opens.

There is **no open choice space yet**.
Resolution is already unfolding.

---

#### **A2. Local perturbation (the greeting arrives)**

* Auditory and social processes register the utterance.
* This introduces *local underdetermination*:

  * something must follow.

However, admissible continuations are already **extremely constrained**:

* greeting ↔ greeting adjacency
* timing expectations
* social norms
* bodily readiness

Crucially:

> **Alternatives never become globally live futures.**

They exist only as abstract possibilities, not as sustained contenders that would have to be borne.

---

#### **A3. Coordination reuse (minimal recruitment)**

* Existing coordination patterns are reused:

  * phonetic templates
  * turn-taking structure
  * motor timing
* Recruitment is minimal.
* No global reorganization is required.

Intrinsic constraint barely rises.

Inertia dominates because nothing else is needed to remain coherent.

---

#### **A4. Settlement during execution**

* One utterance begins forming.
* Competing continuations are never stabilized.
* Switching costs spike immediately because speech has already started.

By the time any “intervention” could occur, the future is already unfolding.

Settlement occurs **during** execution, not before it.

---

#### **A5. After the fact: ownership without choice**

* You hear yourself speak.
* The utterance is:

  * fluent
  * appropriate
  * unmistakably yours
* There is:

  * no felt loss
  * no regret
  * no pressure of responsibility

**Settlement happened.
Choice did not.**

The action is owned because it was borne within closure—
not because it was choice-qualified.

---

### **Walkthrough B: Deliberate Choice (Job Offer)**

*Settlement with choice*

> *You are deciding whether to accept a job offer.*

---

#### **B1. Ongoing regime (before deliberation begins)**

* Life is already in motion:

  * current job
  * financial structure
  * relationships
  * identity commitments
* There is no pause.
* There is no clean slate.

Deliberation begins **inside an already unfolding trajectory**.

---

#### **B2. Alternatives become globally live futures**

Multiple continuations are now **temporarily sustainable**:

* accept
* decline
* negotiate

Each implies:

* different long-horizon coordination
* different identity sedimentation
* different irreversible consequences

These alternatives are not merely imagined.
They are **carried as futures you would have to bear**.

This is the decisive difference.

---

#### **B3. Rising intrinsic constraint**

Sustaining multiple futures is costly:

* recruitment increases
* affective tension rises
* attention narrows
* switching costs begin to diverge

Bias exists but is not decisive:

* pros and cons conflict
* commitments pull in different directions

Settlement pressure increases, but collapse does not yet occur.

---

#### **B4. Overlapping execution and narrowing**

Nothing waits.

While deliberation continues:

* you go to work
* you talk to people
* you sleep poorly
* time passes

Simultaneously:

* one future becomes cheaper to sustain
* others require increasing reorganization
* bias quietly intensifies

Alternatives fade **as time passes**, not at a decision point.

Choice operates **in parallel** with ongoing resolution.

---

#### **B5. Settlement (no moment, just loss)**

Eventually:

* one continuation stabilizes
* others are no longer viable without major disruption
* the regime continues along one path

There is no internal “now I decide.”

What appears instead:

* relief, dread, resolve, or regret
* a sense that “it’s done”
* ownership of consequences

That felt collapse **is** choice.

---

#### **B6. After settlement: irreversibility with authorship**

* History has accumulated.
* Identity has shifted.
* Counterfactuals are inert.
* Responsibility attaches.

Not because you controlled the outcome,
but because **you are the system that must now live in it**.

Same mechanism.
Different geometry.
