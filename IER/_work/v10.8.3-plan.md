# IER v10.8.3 — Part II Completion Plan
**Time-Extended Organization Under Intrinsic Constraint**

---

## Status and Function

This document is a **planning and orientation artifact only**.

It:

* introduces **no ontological, criterial, epistemic, or ethical commitments**
* proposes **no revisions** to the IER Specification
* authorizes **no new claims**
* licenses **no diagnostics, mechanisms, or applications**

All binding authority remains fixed **exclusively** in the existing IER v10.8 corpus.

The purpose of this document is to identify **structural gaps** in Part II and to outline a **non-expansive completion pass** for version **v10.8.3**, focused on **time-extended experiential organization**.

---

## Guiding Principle

**v10.8.3 does not add domains.
It completes diachronic consequences of existing commitments.**

All proposed additions:

* presuppose categorical onset
* presuppose Unified Experiential Field (UEF) identity
* presuppose non-representational intrinsic constraint
* preserve non-diagnosability
* avoid mechanisms, thresholds, or sufficiency claims

The aim is **organizational closure**, not theoretical growth.

---

## Motivating Diagnosis

Part II currently excels at:

* local experiential organization
* regime-preserving maneuvers
* crisis, affect, and ethics-heavy phenomena
* boundary cases (sleep, dormancy, impairment)

However, it remains **underdeveloped** in explaining how experiential systems:

* accumulate structure over time
* carry history without representation
* stabilize skill, habit, and temperament
* prepare for experience prior to categorical onset

These omissions invite reader misinterpretation (e.g., proto-experience, representational memory, graded subjecthood) despite being **structurally incompatible** with IER.

v10.8.3 addresses these gaps directly.

---

## Core Completion Themes (No New Commitments)

### 1. Memory as Structural History (Not Storage)

**Gap Identified**

Memory appears implicitly throughout Part II (continuity, persistence, narrative) but lacks a dedicated structural treatment.

**IER-Compatible Framing**

Memory is treated as:

* constraint-shaping hysteresis
* path-dependence of admissible futures
* structural sedimentation of intrinsic constraint
* history that persists **without stored content**

This treatment:

* blocks representational import
* explains the felt authority of memory without epistemic privilege
* unifies narrative continuity with regime dynamics

**Proposed Outcome**

A single anchor document that stabilizes downstream discussions of skill, habit, learning, and identity.

---

### 2. Development and Maturation as Regime Preparation

**Gap Identified**

IER sharply specifies **when experience exists**, but has limited articulation of how systems become capable of sustaining a UEF.

**IER-Compatible Framing**

Development is described as:

* continuous reorganization of constraint-bearing capacity
* maturation of buffering and global integration
* accumulation of organizational readiness
* preparation for inevitability **without experience attribution**

This is not speculative biology or proto-consciousness.
It is **pre-UEF regime preparation**.

**Proposed Outcome**

A document clarifying infancy → childhood → adulthood trajectories without violating categorical onset.

---

### 3. Skill, Habit, Expertise, and Automaticity

**Gap Identified**

IER explains agency and choice but lacks a structural account of:

* skilled action
* automaticity
* habit formation
* flow states

**IER-Compatible Framing**

Skill is explained as:

* collapse of constraint into narrow, low-cost attractors
* reliable pruning of admissible futures
* reduced global load via stabilized trajectories

Automaticity involves **no sub-agents**, **no unconscious homunculi**, and **no representational scripts**.

**Proposed Outcome**

A document explaining expertise as an experiential *simplification* rather than enrichment.

---

### 4. Long-Horizon Affect, Mood, and Temperament

**Gap Identified**

IER treats acute affect extremely well but under-articulates affective structure across long time horizons.

**IER-Compatible Framing**

Mood and temperament are treated as:

* persistent bias in constraint geometry
* long-horizon attractor structure
* stable modulation of salience and admissible futures

Mood disorders are understood structurally as **persistent constraint skew**, not diagnostic categories.

**Proposed Outcome**

Completion of the affective landscape without importing psychology or clinical frameworks.

---

### 5. Attention as Constraint Allocation (Optional, Integrative)

**Clarificatory Opportunity**

Attention appears across multiple documents but remains diffuse.

**IER-Compatible Framing**

Attention is treated as:

* allocation of intrinsic constraint
* selective participation recruitment
* modulation of global load distribution

Explicitly distinguished from:

* subject creation
* epistemic access
* control or authorship

**Proposed Outcome**

Optional integrative document or subsection tying together memory, skill, and affect.

## What v10.8.3 Explicitly Does *Not* Add

To preserve canonical strictness, v10.8.3 will **not** include:

* neuroscience correlations
* mechanistic explanations
* sufficiency conditions
* diagnostics or indicators
* controllability claims
* engineering guidance
* AI consciousness speculation
* degrees or continua of experience

In particular, the following are explicitly excluded:

* “IER and neuroscience”
* “How to detect a UEF”
* “Building conscious AI”
* “Degrees of consciousness”

These remain structurally incompatible with IER’s identity claim.
