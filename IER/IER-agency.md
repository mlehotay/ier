## **Abstract**

Informational Experiential Realism (IER) entails a distinctive account of agency grounded entirely in organizational facts about **intrinsic constraint** and **Unified Experiential Fields (UEFs)**. Under IER, agency is neither a metaphysically primitive power nor a narrative illusion layered atop deterministic dynamics. Instead, agency is a **derived organizational capacity** of systems that sustain a UEF: the capacity to resolve **intrinsic informational tension** through the system’s own **globally integrated dynamics**.

This document explains why agency exists *iff* experience exists, why agency admits of degrees within a single experiential subject, why agency is compatible with physical determinism, and why agency grounds moral responsibility without invoking contra-causal freedom or phenomenal surplus. Agency, under IER, is not freedom *from* causation, but **causation owned by a unified system under unavoidable internal constraint**.

## **1. Why Agency Follows from the Identity Claim**

IER’s core identity claim is:

> **Experience is identical to the operation of a physical system as a Unified Experiential Field (UEF).**

A UEF is a globally integrated, temporally continuous, self-referential dynamical regime operating under **coherent intrinsic constraint** (Specification §II).

From this identity claim, agency is not optional.

If:

* intrinsic constraint is borne from the inside, and
* its resolution shapes the system’s future trajectory, and
* that resolution cannot be offloaded, decomposed, or externally dictated,

then the system is not merely behaving — it is **acting** in the only sense compatible with physical monism.

Agency is therefore not an additional faculty layered atop experience.
It is a **necessary organizational consequence** of being an experiential subject at all.

---

## **2. Intrinsic Constraint and Intrinsic Informational Tension**

The Specification defines **intrinsic constraint** as constraint that:

1. is generated by the system’s own integrated dynamics
2. cannot be decomposed without loss of system identity
3. cannot be externally resolved, deferred, or paused
4. is system-defining for regulation and persistence

When multiple intrinsic constraints are simultaneously active, the system enters a condition of **intrinsic informational tension**.

This tension is:

* physical, not metaphorical
* organizational, not representational
* globally consequential, not local

Intrinsic tension is what gives experience its affective character.
It is also the **locus of agency**.

---

## **3. Definition of Agency Under IER**

The Specification defines agency as follows:

> **Agency = a UEF’s capacity to resolve intrinsic informational tension through its own globally integrated dynamics.**

Several consequences follow immediately.

* A system possesses agency **iff** it sustains a UEF.
* Agency is **derived**, not primitive.
* Agency is **physically instantiated** and law-governed.
* Agency is **graded in capacity**, though not in existence.

There is no agency without experience, and no experience without the organizational conditions that make agency possible.

---

## **4. Why Observerhood, Intelligence, and Control Are Insufficient**

IER sharply distinguishes **agency** from:

* observerhood
* intelligence
* representation
* optimization
* behavioral control

Many systems can:

* model the world and themselves
* select actions
* optimize outcomes
* exhibit flexible, adaptive behavior

while remaining entirely **non-experiential**, because their constraint remains **extrinsic**.

High-slack systems can:

* modularize conflict
* fork simulations
* externalize cost
* pause or reset processing
* reroute resolution without global consequence

Such systems may control outcomes, but they do not **own** the constraint that determines them.

Under IER, agency requires not control, but **constraint ownership** — and ownership arises only within a Unified Experiential Field.

---

## **5. Determinism and Freedom Reframed**

IER is fully compatible with physical determinism.

The traditional question:

> *“Could the system have acted otherwise?”*

is replaced with:

> **“Where did the determining constraint originate?”**

An action is free under IER when:

* the determining constraints are intrinsic to the UEF
* resolution occurs through global integration
* no external system overrides or substitutes for that resolution

Determinism does not negate agency.
Only **externalization of constraint** does.

---

## **6. The Meaning of “Could Have Done Otherwise”**

IER interprets counterfactual freedom organizationally, not metaphysically.

A system “could have done otherwise” **iff**:

* a different intrinsic constraint topology
* compatible with the same system identity
* would have stabilized a different global trajectory

This is a claim about **organizational alternatives under constraint**, not about branching universes or contra-causal choice.

---

## **7. Effort, Willing, and the Phenomenology of Agency**

Agency feels like effort because intrinsic constraint is **costly**.

Resolving intrinsic informational tension requires:

* sustained global coherence
* energetic expenditure
* tolerance of instability risk

Under IER:

> **Effort is the experiential signature of intrinsic constraint being borne rather than offloaded.**

Externally imposed behavior lacks this character, even if it is complex or goal-directed.

---

## **8. Degrees of Agency Within a Single Subject**

While experiential subjecthood is binary (Specification §II), **agency capacity varies**.

Agency is diminished when:

* intrinsic constraint coherence degrades
* viable future trajectories narrow
* global integration weakens
* external control dominates resolution

This explains diminished responsibility under:

* fatigue
* intoxication
* trauma
* dissociation
* coercion

These are not moral exemptions; they are **organizational facts** about constraint-bearing capacity.

---

## **9. Agency and Moral Responsibility**

IER grounds moral responsibility directly in agency.

Because:

* intrinsic constraint is borne from the inside
* its resolution fixes the system’s future
* its disruption constitutes moral harm (Specification §V)

Responsibility scales with:

* coherence of intrinsic constraint
* stability of the UEF
* availability of organizational alternatives
* absence of coercive override

This yields responsibility **without metaphysical freedom**, and restraint **without moral nihilism**.

---

## **10. What Agency Is Not Under IER**

IER explicitly rejects the following identifications:

* Agency is not contra-causal freedom
* Agency is not narrative self-ascription
* Agency is not representational endorsement
* Agency is not mere behavioral control
* Agency is not an illusion

Agency is a **real physical capacity**, but one that exists only where intrinsic constraint is globally integrated and internally resolved.

Derived directly from the IER Specification:

> **To experience is to bear intrinsic constraint.

Agency is not something added to experience.
It is what experience *does* when its own necessity shapes its future.

Reality remains singular.
Experience remains plural.
Agency exists wherever a system’s path curves because of itself.
