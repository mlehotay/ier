## **1. Orientation: Why “Cognition” Must Be Reframed**

Most theories of mind treat cognition as something layered *on top of* experience:

* information processing over representations
* inference across stored contents
* control exercised by executive modules
* observation performed by meta-levels

Under Informational Experiential Realism, all such framings are structurally unavailable.

IER identifies experience with the operation of a **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)**: a single, globally dominant, intrinsically constrained physical regime. Once such a regime exists, there are:

* no internal observers
* no representational stores
* no separable cognitive subsystems
* no meta-levels standing outside experience

This raises a forced question:

> **If there is only one experiential regime, what can cognition be at all?**

This document answers that question by making explicit what cognition *must* be if the IER identity claim is true.

## **2. The Core Claim: What Cognition Is Under IER**

Under Informational Experiential Realism:

> **Cognition is the reproduction of constraint topologies within a single Unified Experiential Field, at variable participation depths and temporal anchors.**

Cognition is **not**:

* computation
* symbol manipulation
* inference
* simulation
* internal observation
* control over mental parts

It is a **mode of engagement** with already-existing intrinsic constraint.

This claim introduces no new primitives.
It follows necessarily from:

* the identity of experience with a UEF
* the impossibility of multiple experiential regimes
* the structural requirement that thinking, remembering, imagining, and reflecting occur **without** replay, storage, or subject multiplication

## **3. The Minimal Cognitive Machinery (No Additions)**

IER cognition requires **exactly and only** the structures already established elsewhere in the corpus:

1. **Topology reproduction**
   Re-entering the *organizational shape* of constraint without reinstantiating full intrinsic load.

2. **Participation modulation**
   Variable recruitment of processes into intrinsic constraint, without affecting subjecthood.

3. **Gating**
   Limitation of participation to prevent reproduced structures from recruiting the system globally.

4. **Temporal anchoring**
   Selection of basins in admissible constraint space corresponding to past, present, possible, or future organizations.

5. **Attractor and switching dynamics**
   Stability, narrowing, and transition costs that determine how easily the system can leave one organization and enter another.

No further cognitive components are permitted or required.
Any theory that adds more is importing machinery that the IER identity claim disallows.

## **4. Why Faculties Multiply in Other Theories**

Traditional cognitive theories proliferate faculties because they attempt to explain many cognitive modes **without allowing variable participation inside one regime**.

As a result:

* memory becomes *storage*
* imagination becomes *simulation*
* reflection becomes *observation*
* planning becomes *deliberation*
* metacognition becomes *meta-representation*

Each of these moves introduces either:

* dormant experiential content,
* internal observers, or
* parallel experiential processes.

All three violate the IER identity claim.

IER avoids faculty multiplication by treating all cognitive variation as arising from **one operation** — topology reproduction — used at different depths, under different gates, and at different temporal anchors.

## **6. Cognitive Load, Effort, and “Thinking Hard”**

Under IER, cognitive effort is not expenditure of a mental resource.
It is **organizational cost**.

Experiences described as:

* “thinking hard”
* mental fatigue
* overload
* inability to continue

correspond to combinations of:

* high participation recruitment
* attractor narrowing
* increased switching cost
* exhausted buffering capacity

Nothing is being “used up” or “spent.”
What changes is how much intrinsic constraint the system can continue to bear coherently.

This explains why:

* effort feels urgent but non-volitional
* rest restores cognition without adding information
* bodily regulation is often more effective than reasoning

No optimization principle is implied.

## **7. Cognitive Diversity Without Hierarchy**

Because cognition is constraint geometry, not faculty possession, **diversity of cognitive style is expected**.

Differences in:

* imagery availability
* inner speech
* switching speed
* narrative coherence
* tolerance for exploration

reflect **different stable cognitive architectures** within intact Unified Experiential Fields.

These differences:

* do not imply richer or poorer experience
* do not rank experiential standing
* do not track intelligence or correctness

They are **organizational facts**, not deficits.

## **8. Narrative, Self-Modeling, and Autobiographical Thought (Preview)**

One of the most persistent confusions about cognition concerns the “self.”

Under IER:

> **Narrative cognition is not representation of a self.
> It is stabilization of future-directed intrinsic constraint across time.**

Autobiographical thought consists in:

* past-anchored reproduction of prior constraint organizations
* selective retention of low-cost, high-coherence patterns
* use of narrative as an attractor that preserves continuity

The “sense of self” arises when a topology remains **reproducible across time**.

Disruptions of identity do not require subject loss.
They occur when narrative structures cease to be viable under current intrinsic constraint.

A full treatment is deferred to a dedicated article on narrative and selfhood.
