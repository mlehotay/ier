## **1. Orientation: The Missing Verb**

Readers often feel that something is missing between:

* *multiple possible futures*, and
* *one future that actually occurs*.

That missing thing is usually named with a verb:

*choose*, *decide*, *select*, *actualize*, *commit*.

Under Informational Experiential Realism, all such verbs mislead.

They suggest:

* an operation performed on futures,
* a selector applied to alternatives, or
* a moment where reality is “made real.”

IER forbids all three.

The aim of this article is to show that **nothing needs to act** for a future to become fixed. What happens instead is **settlement**: the loss of alternatives under intrinsic constraint.

---

## **2. What Settlement Is (Minimal Definition)**

Under IER:

> **Settlement is the irreversible collapse of admissible futures into a single continuation of a globally coherent regime.**

Settlement is **not**:

* choice
* agency
* decision
* action
* evaluation
* optimization

Settlement is a **structural fact** about how regimes continue through time.

It names *what happens when not all possible continuations can be sustained*.

---

## **3. What Alternatives Are Under IER**

To understand settlement, we must be precise about alternatives.

A **live alternative** is not:

* a representation,
* a simulation,
* a stored plan,
* an imagined branch,
* an epistemic uncertainty.

A live alternative is:

> **A physically admissible continuation of the current regime that remains dynamically supportable under intrinsic constraint.**

“Possibility” here is **physical**, not modal or epistemic.

Alternatives are live only so long as:

* the regime can sustain the coordination patterns they require,
* without destabilizing global coherence.

---

## **4. Why There Is No Edge in Choice Space**

It is tempting to picture settlement as crossing a boundary in a “choice space.”

IER rejects this picture.

There is:

* no discrete edge,
* no threshold event,
* no switch,
* no moment of selection.

Instead:

> **Settlement occurs when a region of viability collapses, not when a boundary is crossed.**

Alternatives disappear because the regime **loses the capacity to sustain them**, not because something eliminates them.

Choice space shrinks by **loss of support**, not by exclusion.

---

## **5. The Physical Basis of Settlement**

What changes physically when settlement occurs is not a decision variable.

What changes is **global coordination**.

As intrinsic constraint rises:

* multiple incompatible coordination patterns compete,
* recruitment concentrates around one pattern,
* switching costs escalate,
* coherence stabilizes asymmetrically.

At a certain point:

> **Only one continuation remains dynamically maintainable.**

At that point, settlement has occurred.

No chooser is required.
No operation is performed.
The regime simply continues as what it can still be.

---

## **6. Irreversibility and the Arrow of Time**

Settlement is inseparable from **irreversibility**.

If all trajectories were reversible:

* alternatives would never truly disappear,
* history would not accumulate,
* futures would not fix.

Under IER:

* the arrow of time is not a selector,
* it is the condition under which alternatives cannot all persist.

Before settlement:

* multiple futures are genuinely admissible.

After settlement:

* only one remains.

This does **not** imply fatalism.

“Could have been otherwise” is:

* **true before settlement**, and
* **false after settlement**.

No contradiction is introduced.

---

## **7. Bias Without Preference**

Settlements are **biased**.

That bias is physical, not evaluative.

Bias arises because:

* existing coordination is reused more easily than reorganized,
* prior structure lowers incremental cost,
* coherence resists radical reconfiguration.

This bias is:

* not preference,
* not desire,
* not value,
* not rational choice.

> **Bias is differential sustainability, not selection.**

Futures that preserve regime coherence at lower incremental cost are simply more viable.

---

## **8. Settlement Without Choice vs. Settlement With Choice**

Not all settlement qualifies as choice.

### **8.1 Settlement Without Choice**

Examples include:

* automatic greetings,
* fluent speech,
* skilled action,
* social adjacency pairs.

In these cases:

* alternatives never become live futures,
* settlement is immediate,
* no narrowing is experienced.

The utterance or action “just comes out” because **the regime is already mid-trajectory**.

---

### **8.2 Settlement With Choice**

Choice occurs when:

* multiple futures are briefly sustained,
* narrowing is experienced as pressure,
* collapse is felt as loss,
* irreversibility is owned.

Choice is therefore:

> **The experience of settlement when bias is weak, conflicted, or unstable.**

Choice does not cause settlement.
Settlement qualifies as choice under certain geometries.

## **9. Where Self and Ownership Enter (Briefly)**

Settlement does not require a self.

Ownership enters because:

* the same Unified Experiential Field continues,
* consequences are borne internally,
* history accumulates within the same regime.

The future is *mine* not because I selected it,
but because **I am the system that must live with it**.

This article does not re-derive ownership or agency.
Those are treated elsewhere.
