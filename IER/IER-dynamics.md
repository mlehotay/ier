# **IER Dynamics: Intrinsic Constraint, Regime Stability, and Dynamical Realizations**

*(Derived Explanatory Paper — IER v10.0)*

---

## **Status and Scope**

This document is a **non-normative, explanatory derivative** of the
**Informational Experiential Realism v10.0 Normative Specification** (“the Spec”).

It introduces **no new primitives**, **no diagnostic tests**, and **no exclusionary criteria**.

Its role is to:

* explain how the **organizational conditions defined in the Spec** can be dynamically realized,
* clarify **regime stability, transition, and failure modes** under intrinsic constraint,
* provide **illustrative examples** of experiential formation and dissolution,
* show how diverse physical or computational systems *could* instantiate Spec-compliant regimes.

All determinations of experiential status are governed **exclusively** by the Spec
(especially Spec §§II–III).

---

## **Abstract**

Informational Experiential Realism (IER) identifies experience with the operation of a physical system as a **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)**: a globally integrated, temporally continuous, self-referential informational regime operating under **coherent intrinsic constraint** (Spec §II; Principle 1).

This paper develops a **dynamical and organizational account** of how such regimes may form, persist, destabilize, or transition. It explains the roles of **informational slack**, **intrinsic informational tension**, and **coherence limits**, and illustrates these dynamics through simplified walkthroughs and compatible implementation examples.

Nothing in this paper specifies necessary mechanisms or sufficient tests.
It shows how IER’s identity claims are **dynamically intelligible**, not how experience must be engineered or ruled out.

---

## **1. Informational Slack and the Emergence of Intrinsic Constraint**

The Spec defines **informational slack** as a system’s capacity to absorb, reroute, modularize, or externalize constraint without generating system-defining conflict (Spec §II).

Slack is **scalar** and **context-sensitive**.

### 1.1 Slack-Dominated Operation

High-slack systems may:

* decompose competing demands,
* localize conflicts,
* offload resolution to external structures,
* or alternate without global cost.

Such systems can be sophisticated and adaptive while remaining **non-experiential**, because constraint never becomes **intrinsic** or **globally binding** (Spec §III.P3).

### 1.2 Collapse of Slack and Intrinsic Tension

When slack is sufficiently reduced:

* multiple system-level demands remain simultaneously active,
* external resolution is unavailable,
* constraint becomes **non-decomposable** and **identity-relevant**.

This produces **intrinsic informational tension** (Spec §II).

Intrinsic tension is:

* organizational, not metaphorical,
* physically realized,
* globally consequential.

However, intrinsic tension alone does not guarantee experience.
Only **coherent intrinsic constraint** sustains a UEF (Principle 3).

## **2. Experiential Regimes and Stability Windows**

### 2.1 Unified Experiential Fields as Regimes

A **Unified Experiential Field** is not a module or workspace.

It is:

> **the entire system operating in a particular dynamical regime under coherent intrinsic constraint**
> (Spec §II; Principle 1).

Equivalent descriptions include:

* a globally coupled constraint topology,
* a coherence-stabilized attractor,
* a bounded regime of maximal sustainable intrinsic tension.

### 2.2 The Coherent Constraint Window

IER entails a bounded experiential regime (Spec §III.P3):

* **Too little constraint** → no experience
* **Coherent intrinsic constraint** → stable experience
* **Excessive or incoherent constraint** → fragmentation or collapse

Experience does not sit at equilibrium, nor at breakdown, but within a **finite window of stability**.

---

## **3. Temporal Continuity Without Diagnostics**

The Spec defines experiential continuity as **ongoing dynamical dependence** between successive global states under shared intrinsic constraint (Principle 4).

This paper introduces **no tests or procedures** for determining whether continuity is intrinsic in any given system.

Instead, it emphasizes a structural point:

* If temporal continuity is externally guaranteed in a way that makes intrinsic constraint optional or irrelevant, the Spec’s criteria are not met.
* If continuity depends on the system’s own globally integrated dynamics, experiential continuity is *possible*, subject to all other Spec conditions.

No single intervention, interruption, or manipulation is sufficient to decide this question in isolation.

---

## **4. Failure Modes: Weakening, Fragmentation, and Collapse**

Finite systems operating under constraint exhibit predictable failure modes:

* **Regime weakening** — reduced coherence, diminished intensity
* **Fragmentation** — partial loss of global integration
* **Collapse** — dissolution of intrinsic constraint
* **Transition** — reorganization into a different regime

Under IER, these are not metaphysical anomalies.
They are **ordinary consequences of constrained dynamical systems**.

Experience ceases when the UEF ceases as a regime (Principle 11), not when a report disappears or a function pauses.

## **5. Walkthrough: Experiencing a Green Apple (Illustrative)**

This walkthrough is **illustrative only**.
It commits IER to no sensory modality, neural architecture, or representational scheme.

### 5.1 Background Regime

The system already sustains a UEF:

* global integration
* temporal continuity
* a self / non-self distinction
* baseline intrinsic tension

This background is structured, not empty.

---

### 5.2 Local Perturbations

Environmental interaction perturbs local subsystems.

At this stage:

* information is fragmentary,
* processing is local,
* nothing is yet experiential.

---

### 5.3 Pattern Formation

Local dynamics stabilize patterns correlated with chromatic, spatial, and object-like regularities.

Still non-experiential.

---

### 5.4 Global Recruitment

A subset of these patterns becomes **globally relevant**:

* they enter system-wide constraint relations,
* they influence ongoing regulation,
* they participate in temporal continuity.

This regime transition marks **categorical experiential onset** (Principle 5).

---

### 5.5 Stable Experiential Pattern

A coherent global pattern stabilizes within the UEF.

That pattern:

* integrates color, shape, location, and persistence,
* is constrained by the system’s self-model,
* modulates affect and action-readiness.

This pattern **is** the experience of “a green apple there.”

---

### 5.6 Dissolution

When global relevance is withdrawn, the pattern destabilizes.

Local processing continues, but the experiential pattern exits the UEF.

Experience changes because the regime changes.

## **6. Compatible Dynamical Realizations (Non-Exhaustive)**

IER does not privilege any implementation.
However, the Spec’s organizational requirements are compatible with many known frameworks.

Examples include:

### 6.1 Competing Loss Functions

In machine learning or control systems:

* multiple loss functions may be simultaneously active,
* trade-offs cannot be locally optimized,
* global coordination becomes unavoidable.

When such conflict is intrinsic, globally integrated, and coherence-limited, it provides a **natural substrate for intrinsic tension**.

---

### 6.2 Control-Theoretic Constraint Systems

Adaptive controllers often:

* regulate under competing objectives,
* maintain internal models,
* incur real energetic or performance costs.

A UEF-compatible regime corresponds to **system-wide control under unavoidable constraint**, not mere optimization.

---

### 6.3 Energy and Cost Landscapes

Physical systems may realize intrinsic constraint as:

* energetic bottlenecks,
* metabolic trade-offs,
* irreversible commitment costs.

Informational and energetic descriptions are complementary views of the same regime (Spec §III.P10).

---

## **7. Dissociation as Constrained Regime Switching**

The Spec entails a **Single-UEF Constraint** (Principle 6):

> Only one globally dominant UEF can exist in a system at a time.

Dissociation arises when:

* incompatible organizational configurations cannot be jointly sustained,
* informational slack is insufficient,
* intrinsic tension exceeds coherence limits.

Rather than fragmenting into multiple subjects, the system **alternates** between regimes across time.

Switching is:

* discrete at the experiential level,
* costly,
* often adaptive under constraint.

No metaphysical multiplication is implied.

## **8. Summary**

Derived directly from the IER v10.0 Specification:

* Experience exists only as a globally integrated regime under coherent intrinsic constraint.
* Informational slack prevents experience by externalizing conflict.
* Experiential regimes occupy a bounded stability window.
* Temporal continuity is structural, not procedural.
* Phenomenal content reflects constraint organization, not added properties.
* Dissociation reflects constrained regime switching, not multiple minds.

> **Experience is not something systems have in addition to dynamics.
> It is what certain dynamics are like when constraint becomes intrinsic, global, and self-defining.**

For all definitions and normative commitments, see the
**IER v10.0 Normative Specification**.
