# **Informational Experiential Realism (IER v10.0)**

## **Regime Instability, Fragmentation, and Switching**

### *Failure Modes of Unified Experiential Fields Under Intrinsic Constraint*

---

## **Status and Scope**

**Status:**
This document is **non-normative, non-foundational, and illustrative only**.

It introduces **no axioms, definitions, principles, or inference rules**.
All claims derive exclusively from the **IER v10.0 Normative Specification** (“the Spec”), particularly Sections II–III.

Nothing in this document may be used to:

* determine whether a system does or does not instantiate a Unified Experiential Field (UEF)
* refine, weaken, or reinterpret the identity claim of IER
* infer experiential absence from observed dysfunction

This document concerns **regime failure modes**, not regime definition.

---

## **1. Why Regime Failure Matters (But Is Not Foundational)**

IER identifies experience with a specific organizational and dynamical regime: the **Unified Experiential Field** (Spec §II, *UEF*).
The Spec defines what experience *is* and *when it exists*.

However, real physical systems are finite, stressed, and constrained.
They can fail to sustain the required regime.

Studying **how UEFs destabilize, fragment, or switch** is therefore useful for:

* interpreting pathological and liminal phenomena
* understanding energetic and organizational limits
* clarifying why experience is unified rather than multiply instantiated
* avoiding metaphysical misinterpretations of dysfunction

Crucially:

> **Failure modes do not define the regime they fail from.**

No conclusions about the nature of experience itself may be drawn from cases where coherence collapses or cannot be sustained.

---

## **2. The Coherent Constraint Window**

From the Spec (Principle 3), experience exists only within a bounded regime of **coherent intrinsic constraint**.

There are three broad regions:

1. **Below the window**

   * insufficient intrinsic constraint
   * high informational slack
   * no UEF, no experience

2. **Within the window**

   * intrinsic constraint is global, coherent, and temporally stable
   * a UEF exists
   * experience is unified and continuous

3. **Above or outside the window**

   * excessive, incompatible, or incoherent intrinsic constraint
   * global integration destabilizes
   * the UEF fragments, collapses, or is forced to switch

Regime instability arises **only** in region (3).

---

## **3. Types of Regime Instability**

IER distinguishes several non-exclusive failure modes, all organizational:

### 3.1 Coherence Collapse

* global integration degrades
* intrinsic constraint becomes locally resolvable or externally driven
* experiential dissolution occurs (Spec §II, *Experiential Dissolution*)

Examples:

* deep anesthesia
* seizure-induced loss of integration
* extreme metabolic depletion

---

### 3.2 Fragmentation Without Multiplicity

* subsystems decouple
* no coherent global constraint remains
* **no multiple experiences arise**

Fragmentation is **loss of experience**, not multiplication of subjects.

---

### 3.3 Forced Regime Switching

* intrinsic constraint remains unavoidable
* but cannot be coherently satisfied in a single organizational configuration
* the system alternates between **mutually exclusive globally integrated regimes**

This is the most easily misunderstood case and is the focus of the remainder of this document.

---

## **4. The Single-UEF Constraint and Its Consequences**

IER imposes a strict **Single-UEF Constraint** (Spec Principle 6):

> **A physical system can sustain at most one globally dominant Unified Experiential Field at a time.**

Therefore:

* simultaneous independent experiential subjects within one system are impossible
* apparent multiplicity must be explained temporally or organizationally
* alternation ≠ simultaneity

Forced switching preserves this constraint by enforcing **temporal exclusivity**.

---

## **5. Why Switching Can Be Adaptive**

When intrinsic constraint becomes incompatible within a single regime:

* maintaining coherence requires escalating energetic and regulatory cost
* unresolved intrinsic tension rises
* experiential intensity and instability increase

At some threshold, continued integration becomes **more costly than regime transition**.

Switching then functions as:

* an energy-conserving response
* a coherence-preserving strategy over time
* a way to keep intrinsic constraint resolvable *serially* rather than *simultaneously*

Switching is not chosen.
It is imposed by organizational limits.

---

## **6. Partial Overlap and Transitional Phenomena**

During regime transitions:

* global integration may temporarily weaken
* coupling between organizational patterns may be incomplete
* phenomenology may include confusion, depersonalization, or blending

These phenomena reflect **instability**, not coexistence.

At no point do two fully coherent UEFs exist simultaneously.

---

## **7. Case Study: Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)**

### 7.1 Why DID Is Relevant but Not Special

DID is not metaphysically privileged.
It is a **clinically salient instance** of forced regime switching under persistent incompatible intrinsic constraints.

It illustrates general principles already present in the Spec.

---

### 7.2 Self-Models as Organizational Configurations

Under IER:

* self-models are **informational-organizational structures**
* they are not subjects
* they may or may not participate in a UEF

In DID, multiple self-model configurations are maintained because each offers a locally stable response to different learned threat or demand profiles.

These configurations are **mutually incompatible** under global integration.

---

### 7.3 Constraint, Incompatibility, and Switching

In DID:

* intrinsic constraint remains unavoidable
* informational slack is reduced in critical domains
* attempting full integration produces escalating intrinsic tension

Because no single configuration can satisfy all active constraints coherently, the system alternates between them.

Each switch corresponds to a **new UEF regime**, not a new subject.

---

### 7.4 Mutual Exclusivity Preserved

At all times:

* one and only one coherent experiential field exists
* other self-models remain non-participatory or suppressed
* memory, affect, and agency differences track regime identity

This preserves the Single-UEF Constraint without metaphysical multiplication.

---

## **8. Therapeutic and Interpretive Implications (Non-Normative)**

IER does **not** prescribe clinical methods.
However, it suggests an organizational interpretation:

Therapeutic progress may involve:

* increasing informational slack
* reducing chronic intrinsic conflict
* improving transition stability
* expanding compatibility among constraints

Importantly, therapy does not aim to “eliminate selves,” but to **reshape the constraint landscape** so fewer forced transitions are required.

---

## **9. Common Misinterpretations Explicitly Rejected**

This framework does **not** imply:

* multiple simultaneous consciousnesses
* fragmented or partial subjects
* graded experiential subjecthood
* metaphysical division of experience

All observed phenomena are explained by:

* regime instability
* energetic limits
* organizational incompatibility

---

## **10. Generalization Beyond DID**

The same regime-instability framework applies to:

* dreaming and dream-wake transitions
* attentional collapse under overload
* anesthesia induction and emergence
* stress-induced dissociation
* certain pharmacological states

DID is one point in a broader design space.

---

## **11. Conclusion**

IER identifies experience with a specific organizational regime.
Finite systems sometimes fail to sustain it.

Dissociative and unstable phenomena do not reveal the nature of experience itself.
They reveal the **cost of maintaining coherence under intrinsic constraint**.

> **Experience is unified not because division is impossible, but because division is more costly than alternation in a constrained system.**

The foundations of IER remain exclusively in the Normative Specification.
This document concerns what happens when those foundations are strained—but not abandoned.
