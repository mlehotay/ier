## **1. Orientation: Why Nothing Ever Stops**

Physical systems do not pause.

They do not wait for deliberation.
They do not suspend while alternatives are considered.
They do not halt to ask what should happen next.

States succeed states.
Trajectories continue.
Resolution unfolds.

This is true of rocks, storms, markets, institutions, and organisms.
It is also true **inside experience**, when experience exists.

The question IER addresses here is not *why motion exists*, but:

> **Why continuation is the default condition, and why interruption requires additional work.**

That default is called **inertia**.

---

## **2. What Inertia Is (IER Definition)**

Under Informational Experiential Realism:

> **Inertia is the persistence of globally coordinated constraint in the absence of additional intrinsic load.**

This definition is structural, not psychological.

Inertia refers to:

* the continuation of an already coherent regime
* reuse of existing coordination
* avoidance of costly reorganization
* persistence until coherence would otherwise fail

Inertia is not something a system *chooses*.
It is what happens **when nothing else intervenes**.

---

## **3. What Inertia Is Not**

Because the word “inertia” is heavily overused, IER explicitly rejects several common misreadings.

Inertia is **not**:

* habit
* laziness
* resistance to change
* preference
* intention
* equilibrium
* passivity

In particular:

* Inertia does **not** imply stasis — systems in inertia can be highly dynamic.
* Inertia does **not** imply lack of ownership — inertia can operate fully within closure.
* Inertia does **not** imply agency failure — it is the baseline against which agency-like phenomena arise.

Newtonian metaphors (“objects resist motion”) are **at best analogies**, not explanatory models.

---

## **4. Inertia as a Constraint Threshold**

The defining feature of inertia is **cost asymmetry**.

Maintaining existing coordination is cheaper than reorganizing it.

As long as:

* global coherence is preserved, and
* no additional intrinsic constraint is required,

the regime continues.

Interruption, redirection, or branching requires:

* recruitment of additional processes
* tolerance of temporary instability
* acceptance of increased intrinsic constraint

This is why:

* actions complete themselves once begun
* speech continues to its grammatical end
* movements follow through
* conversations drift rather than reset

Inertia persists **until coherence would otherwise fail**.

## **5. Inertia and Time**

IER does not treat time as a container, sequence, or set of instants.

Time arises from **irreversible constraint resolution**.

Inertia explains why this resolution:

* does not pause
* does not reset
* does not “wait” for a decision point

There is no special temporal operator required to keep time moving forward.

> **Continuation is cheaper than reorganization.**

That fact alone is sufficient to explain why time feels like it flows rather than flickers.

## **6. Inertia in Experiential Regimes**

Inertia operates **inside Unified Experiential Fields (UEFs)** just as it does elsewhere.

Within experience, inertia explains:

* fluent action
* automatic responses
* completion of thoughts
* persistence of affective tone
* default behavioral trajectories

Importantly:

> Inertia does **not** imply absence of ownership.

An action can be:

* fully owned (borne under closure),
* fully experiential,
* and entirely inertial.

This is why many actions feel **effortless yet still “mine.”**

---

## **7. Inertia, Fatigue, and Collapse**

Accepting additional intrinsic constraint is costly.

Fatigue, stress, injury, or overload reduce a system’s capacity to bear that cost.

When capacity drops:

* inertia reasserts dominance
* default trajectories resume
* alternatives collapse earlier
* choice-like phenomena disappear

This is not weakness.
It is structural inevitability.

Inertia explains why:

* people revert under stress
* habits resurface under exhaustion
* long-horizon plans collapse late in the day
* interruption feels destabilizing

No motivational story is required.

---

## **8. Inertia Across Domains**

Inertia is not specific to individual human agents.

It operates in:

* relationships (coordination persists)
* families (roles sediment)
* institutions (procedures continue)
* traditions (practices endure)
* markets (momentum dominates)
* propaganda systems (default narratives stick)

In all cases:

* inertia explains persistence
* without attributing agency
* without positing shared subjects
* without invoking intention

Inertia is a **regime-level property**, not a personal one.

---

## **9. Relation to Bias, Settlement, and Choice**

Inertia must be distinguished from nearby concepts:

* **Inertia** explains *why continuation happens by default*
* **Bias** explains *why some alternatives become unsustainable earlier*
* **Settlement** explains *how alternatives are lost*
* **Choice** explains *what happens when additional constraint is accepted early enough to reshape bias*

Choice does not replace inertia.
It competes with it.
