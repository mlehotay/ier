## **Abstract**

Experiential life exhibits striking regularities. Certain emotions recur. Certain narratives reassert themselves. Certain meanings feel “sticky,” resistant to revision even when circumstances change. These patterns are often explained in terms of learning, representation, preference, or psychological bias.

Under **Informational Experiential Realism (IER)**, these explanations are incomplete.

This article introduces the concept of **soft attractors in experience**: dynamically stable organizations of intrinsic constraint that recur within an already-existing **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)** because they *stabilize global coherence under constraint*. Soft attractors are not representations, values, goals, or meanings encoded by the system. They are **structural regularities** that persist because alternatives are dynamically unstable or costly.

The account introduces no new primitives, criteria, or diagnostics. All claims are strictly derived from IER’s identity claim, its account of intrinsic constraint, participation modulation, and regime stability.

## **1. The Problem: Why Experience Repeats Itself**

Human experience is not random.

Across individuals and cultures, we observe:

* recurring emotional configurations (fear, grief, longing, relief)
* recurring narrative forms (threat → struggle → resolution)
* recurring identity structures (“who I am,” “what matters,” “what went wrong”)
* recurring symbolic motifs (circles, balance, ascent, descent, return)

These recurrences persist even when:

* individuals try to change them
* evidence contradicts them
* environments shift
* reflection intervenes

This persistence tempts explanation in terms of **preference**, **belief**, or **meaning**.

IER rejects those as primitives.

The recurrence is **structural**.

## **2. From Physical Attractors to Experiential Attractors**

In dynamical systems, an **attractor** is a region of state space toward which trajectories converge because alternative trajectories are unstable.

Attractors do not:

* represent outcomes
* encode goals
* express intentions

They describe **what survives under constraint**.

IER extends this logic to experience — carefully.

A **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)** is already a globally integrated, temporally continuous regime under intrinsic constraint. Once such a regime exists, its internal dynamics are not neutral. Some organizations of participation and constraint are far more stable than others.

These stable organizations are **soft attractors**.

They are “soft” because:

* they do not rigidly determine experience
* they admit variation and modulation
* they can be exited under sufficient perturbation

But they are attractors nonetheless.

## **3. What a Soft Attractor Is (and Is Not)**

### **3.1 What It Is**

A **soft attractor** is:

> a recurring, dynamically stable organization of intrinsic constraint within an already-existing Unified Experiential Field, sustained because it preserves global coherence under ongoing load.

Soft attractors:

* shape experiential content
* bias future participation
* resist disruption
* recur across time and context

---

### **3.2 What It Is Not**

A soft attractor is **not**:

* a representation
* a belief
* a preference
* a goal
* a value
* a message
* a symbol with intrinsic meaning

Nothing is “stored.”
Nothing is “chosen.”
Nothing is “about” anything by necessity.

The attractor persists because **leaving it is costly**.

## **4. Examples of Soft Attractors**

### **4.1 Emotional Attractors**

Emotions often recur in recognizable forms.

Under IER, emotions are not signals or judgments. They are **constraint organizations**.

For example:

* Fear organizes attention narrowly, reducing choice space
* Grief slows temporal dynamics, stabilizing loss integration
* Relief rapidly releases intrinsic tension after sustained constraint

These configurations recur because:

* they stabilize global integration
* they prevent fragmentation
* they localize load
* they preserve continuity

They are attractors because **other organizations fail under the same pressures**.

---

### **4.2 Narrative Attractors**

Narratives recur because they are **constraint-managing trajectories**.

A minimal narrative attractor often looks like:

1. rising intrinsic tension
2. narrowing of viable futures
3. decisive transition
4. partial resolution

This shape reappears in:

* personal memory
* dreams
* myths
* therapy narratives
* cultural stories

Not because the mind prefers stories — but because **constraint over time tends to organize this way**.

Narrative is what constraint looks like when stretched across temporal continuity.

---

### **4.3 Identity Attractors**

Identity feels persistent even when beliefs change.

Under IER, identity persistence reflects:

* stable participation patterns
* recurring constraint resolutions
* familiar load-bearing configurations

Certain self-models recur because abandoning them would:

* destabilize coherence
* require global reorganization
* increase intrinsic cost

Identity is not a stored self.
It is an **attractor basin**.

---

### **4.4 Aesthetic and Symbolic Attractors**

Certain forms feel meaningful or beautiful across cultures.

Under IER:

* beauty is the felt signature of unusually coherent intrinsic constraint
* symbols persist because they stabilize experiential organization

Circles, spirals, balance, symmetry — these recur because they are **low-conflict organizations of constraint**, not because they encode universal meaning.

## **5. Why Soft Attractors Feel Inevitable**

Soft attractors often feel:

* obvious
* necessary
* “how things really are”
* resistant to doubt

This **felt inevitability** is a structural effect.

Within a UEF:

* leaving the attractor destabilizes coherence
* destabilization increases intrinsic cost
* increased cost recruits corrective dynamics
* corrective dynamics push the system back

The experience feels authoritative.

IER insists: **this authority is organizational, not epistemic**.

The attractor stabilizes the system.
It does not reveal truth.

---

## **6. Change, Therapy, and Disruption**

Change is possible — but costly.

Exiting a soft attractor requires:

* sustained perturbation
* alternative stabilizing structures
* tolerance of temporary instability
* preservation of global integration during reorganization

This explains why:

* insight alone often fails
* change feels exhausting
* relapse is common
* stable alternatives matter more than critique

Soft attractors dissolve only when **another configuration becomes cheaper**.

## **7. Ethical and Epistemic Boundaries (Clarification)**

This article does **not** imply that:

* recurring experiences are justified
* stable meanings are true
* inevitability licenses harm
* coherence overrides evidence

IER explicitly rejects experiential authority over truth.

Soft attractors explain **why something feels unavoidable**, not **why it should be trusted**.

Under Informational Experiential Realism:

* Experience unfolds within a Unified Experiential Field
* Within that field, some organizations of constraint are dynamically stable
* These organizations recur as **soft attractors**
* Emotions, narratives, identities, and symbols persist because they stabilize coherence
* Nothing is represented, chosen, or encoded as meaning by necessity
* Felt inevitability reflects organizational cost, not epistemic truth
