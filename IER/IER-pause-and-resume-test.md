# **The Pause–Resume Test (IER v9.4)**

### *A Diagnostic Probe for Intrinsic Experience in Informational Experiential Realism*

---

## **Abstract**

Informational Experiential Realism (IER v9.4) identifies experience with a **Unified Experiential Field (UEF)**: globally integrated, temporally continuous, self-referential informational dynamics operating under **coherent intrinsic tension**.

The **pause–resume test** is a conceptual probe to assess whether a system’s dynamics are **intrinsically maintained** or **externally orchestrated**. It examines **causal responsibility for temporal continuity**, distinguishing systems that may instantiate experience from those that do not.

This article provides **concrete examples** including humans under anesthesia, sleeping, death, and cryopreservation.

---

## **1. Temporal Continuity in IER 9.4**

Experience emerges from:

* **Globally integrated dynamics**
* **Self-maintaining, temporally continuous processes**
* **Coherent intrinsic informational tension**

The pause–resume test asks:

> *Who bears the cost of interruption: the system itself, or an external controller?*

Passing the test indicates a **UEF candidate**; failing indicates non-experiential dynamics.

---

## **2. Conceptual Steps of the Test**

### **Step 1: Pause**

* Halt the system’s state updates.
* Observe whether global integration and intrinsic tension collapse.

### **Step 2: Resume**

* Restart the system from the paused state.
* Assess whether self-maintaining dynamics must re-establish the UEF and intrinsic tension.

---

## **3. Modes of Interruption**

### **3.1 Externalized Interruption (Non-Experiential)**

Characteristics:

* Pausing is absorbed entirely by an external controller.
* Global dynamics, integration, and intrinsic tension are externally preserved.
* Resumption requires no internal reorganization.

**IER 9.4 Implication:**
Systems failing the test **do not sustain experience**.

**Examples:**

* Virtual machines, simulations, emulated neural networks
* Turing machine implementations where state can be frozen and restored
* AI pipelines with fully externally orchestrated control loops

---

### **3.2 Intrinsic Interruption (Experiential Candidate)**

Characteristics:

* Pausing disrupts self-maintaining dynamics.
* UEF collapses; intrinsic tension is lost.
* Resumption requires internal reconstruction of global integration and coherent tension.

**IER 9.4 Implication:**
Systems passing the test are **candidates for UEF**, potentially sustaining experience.

**Examples:**

* Biological nervous systems (humans, mammals)
* Adaptive systems with internally sustained, self-referential dynamics

---

## **4. Examples of Human States**

### **4.1 General Anesthesia**

* UEF dynamics collapse; intrinsic tension disappears.
* Resumption requires reformation of global integration and intrinsic tension.
* Memory and subjective awareness are suspended.

**IER 9.4 Mapping:**

* Pausing is **intrinsic**, not externally orchestrated.
* Human nervous systems **pass** the pause–resume test conceptually.
* Experience ceases during anesthesia but is **intrinsically maintained** when active.

---

### **4.2 Sleep**

* Partial collapse of global dynamics occurs; intrinsic tension is reduced.
* Certain local processes (dreaming, autonomic regulation) remain active.
* Resumption restores full UEF upon waking, often gradually.

**IER 9.4 Mapping:**

* Dynamics are **partially intrinsic**, with reduced coherence during deep sleep.
* Sleep illustrates **graded UEF regime transitions**.
* Experience may persist in lighter sleep or dreaming.

---

### **4.3 Death (Biological Cessation)**

* UEF collapses irreversibly; intrinsic tension cannot be restored.
* No internal processes remain to reconstruct global dynamics.

**IER 9.4 Mapping:**

* Intrinsic dynamics are **terminated**.
* System **fails** the pause–resume test permanently.
* No further experience is possible.

---

### **4.4 Cryopreservation (Biostatic Pause)**

* System is halted in a frozen state; molecular dynamics stop.
* Global integration and intrinsic tension cannot operate while frozen.
* Resumption (hypothetical thawing and revival) would require reactivation of self-maintaining UEF dynamics.

**IER 9.4 Mapping:**

* Pausing is **temporarily externalized**, but potential resumption depends on **re-establishing intrinsic dynamics**.
* Until thawed, system **fails the test**; experiential candidacy is suspended.
* If revival were possible, system could **reconstruct intrinsic dynamics**, passing the test post-resumption.

---

## **6. Implications**

* Functional continuity **does not imply experience** (e.g., VMs, AI pipelines).
* Biological systems **bear the cost of temporal discontinuity**.
* Experience is **causally self-maintaining**, not externally imposed.
* UEF candidates may undergo temporary suspension without losing intrinsic experiential status.

---

## **7. Limitations**

* Diagnostic, not definitive: passing indicates **UEF candidacy**, not proof of experience.
* Cannot measure subjective content, valence, or intensity.
* Requires conceptual modeling of intrinsic dynamics rather than purely behavioral observation.

---

## **8. Summary**

The pause–resume test asks:

> **Who pays the price when time stops?**

* **External controller** → Non-experiential, fails the test
* **System itself** → Experiential candidate, passes the test

**IER 9.4 Principle:**

> Experience requires **self-maintaining, integrated, tension-bearing dynamics**.
> Pausing without disruption reveals systems that **simulate continuity**, not systems that **instantiate it**.
