## Overview

**Informational Experiential Realism (IER)** is a realist theory of experience.

It holds that experience is **neither fundamental nor illusory**, but is instead **identical to a specific organizational and dynamical regime** instantiated by certain physical systems.

Under IER:

> **Experience is what globally integrated informational dynamics are like when they become the dominant, unavoidable constraint on themselves over time.**

IER is:

* physically monist
* non-dualist
* non-panpsychist
* non-eliminativist

It affirms **one objective physical reality** and **many real experiential worlds**, without introducing new substances, properties, or metaphysical domains.

## What IER Does *Not* Do

IER does not claim that:

* experience is fundamental
* all systems experience
* intelligence implies consciousness
* experience violates physical law
* mathematics can diagnose moral status
* ethics requires metaphysical free will

IER identifies experience with **a specific physical regime**, nothing more and nothing less.
